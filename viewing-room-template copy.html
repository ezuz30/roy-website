I.
Arrival
New Year's Eve
Berlin, Germany — 2023
I arrived on a Tuesday in late October. The city was quieter than I remembered — fewer tourists, longer shadows. I didn't press the shutter for three days. Just walked. Just watched. Berlin doesn't give you its photographs. You have to earn them. — October 22, 2023
Sunflower Kiss — Berlin, 2023
They were standing at the corner of Torstraße, completely unaware of anyone else. She was holding sunflowers. He leaned in. I had exactly half a second. — October 24, 2023
This is the frame that defined the entire trip. Everything before it was warm-up. Everything after was trying to match it. — Looking back, January 2024
II.
The Stranger
The Blue Suit
Berlin, Germany — 2023
"He walked like someone who had nowhere to be and everywhere to go."
I found him at Alexanderplatz. The blue suit caught every shift of afternoon light. He never looked at the camera. He didn't need to. Some subjects give you everything just by existing in their own world. — November 8, 2023
The bridge near Kreuzberg became my anchor point. Every evening at 4:30 the light cut through the arches. I returned seven times. Only one frame survived. — November 3, 2023
The Bridge — Girona, 2023

Valencia, Spain — 2023
The Watchman — Girona, 2023
The man on the bench was the last photograph of the trip. He sat in a pool of light so precise it looked staged. It wasn't. That's the thing about street photography — the world composes itself. You just have to be patient enough to notice. — November 15, 2023
