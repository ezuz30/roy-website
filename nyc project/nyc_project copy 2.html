New York is an endless collision of quiet moments in the loudest city on earth.
Here, light doesn't just fall — it fights its way through the concrete.

A Quiet Second
on Canal Street

The crowd parted for half a breath. Steam curled from a grate, catching the last amber light — and for a fraction of a second, everything held still.

I came to photograph buildings, but the city gave me its people instead. Every stranger carried a story written in the way they walked through light.

— Roy Ezuz, New York 2024
